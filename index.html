Ivan Pavlovych Puluj

was a Ukrainian physicist and inventor known for his early research into X-rays. His contributions were largely neglected until the end of the 20th century.

Here's a time line of Ivan Pulyuy's life:

  • February 2, 1845 — Born in the village of Hrymailiv (now Ternopil Oblast, Ukraine) into a family of wealthy peasants.
  • 1864 — Graduated from the Ternopil Gymnasium.
  • 1865 — Entered the University of Vienna, where he studied physics and mathematics.
  • 1872 — Received a doctorate from the University of Vienna.
  • 1874 - 1875 — Worked in the Hittorf laboratory in Strasbourg, engaged in research on cathode rays.
  • 1876 — Defended his doctoral dissertation, which concerned thermodynamics.
  • 1884 — Received a professorship at the German Higher Technical School in Prague.
  • 1892 — Developed one of the first designs of X-ray tubes, which became the basis for radiography.
  • 1896 — Gave public lectures on the discovery of X-rays, pointing to his early experiments in this field.
  • 1902 — Published a Ukrainian translation of the Bible, which became a significant contribution to Ukrainian culture.
  • 1918 — Retired.
  • January 31, 1918 — Died in Prague, buried in the Olšany cemetery.
